Mahalia Thomas

M.A. Candidate at New York University

I'm Mahalia, a current student at New York University focusing on Comparative Politics. As an undergraduate, I was primarily interested in American politics with minors in sociology and public policy.

My research focus has since shifted towards comparative constitutional law and its implications on electoral politics. Current research seeks to explain the rise of global populism within a constitutional framework starting with Germany's 2017 election.

Research Projects

Throughout my time at NYU, I've had numerous opportunities to work with faculty in the Politics Department on both qualitative and quantiative research project. Check them out below:

Instant Runoff Voting: The Monotonicity-Strategic Voting Connection (2019)

Extensive literature review looking at the the Instant Runoff Voting debate, concluding that further work needs to undertaken to understand the relationship between monotonicity and strategic voting.

Freedom to Hate?: Comparative Constitutional Look at Hate Speech (2019)

Quantiative analysis of 50 constitutions and the opportunities they provide for the passing of Hate Speech regulations.

Militant vs. Capitalistic Democracy: The Rise of the AfD through Constitutional Precedence (2019)
